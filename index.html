T&T Resort Phu Quoc

Where Sunset Meets Wellness & Adventure

Executive Summary

T&T Resort, located on beautiful Phu Quoc Island, combines leisure, wellness, and adventure in a boutique lifestyle destination. Our vision includes a rooftop cocktail bar with live sunset music, a wellness-focused spa, health shop, restaurant, and an integrated tour company — TNT Tours. The resort targets the growing market for immersive, wellness, and experiential travel.

Prime location just 200m from the beach, ideal for breathtaking sunsets and easy access to Long Beach and the vibrant town center.

Phu Quoc Island: Growth & Upcoming APEC 2027

Phu Quoc has rapidly transformed into Vietnam's premier island destination, with tourist arrivals growing exponentially over the past decade. The government’s investments in infrastructure and international flights have fueled this boom.

The upcoming APEC Summit 2027 is set to bring unprecedented global attention and increased tourism, making Phu Quoc a hotspot for investors and travelers alike.

This growth presents a perfect opportunity for T&T Resort to capitalize on rising demand for quality accommodations, wellness experiences, and curated tours.

Business Model Components

1. Rooftop Cocktail Bar (high season only): Open 5 PM–11 PM featuring themed cocktails, snacks, and live music during sunset.

2. Wellness Zone: Spa treatments, health-focused restaurant, and wellness shop using authentic Vietnamese products to maintain affordability and quality.

3. TNT Tours: Curated island tours including BBQ boat charters, snorkeling, and private adventures.

4. Guest Rooms: Boutique rooms offered at 500k–600k VND/night low season, 800k–1 million VND/night high season, targeting mid-range to luxury tourists.

Staffing & Compensation

Initially staffed with 3 full-time employees:

  • Manager/Receptionist handling bookings and front desk operations.
  • Bar/Kitchen Lead for rooftop cocktail bar and restaurant.
  • Spa Lead responsible for treatments and wellness services.

Staff are paid a competitive salary of 6–8 million VND/month plus 5–10% commission on sales.

Volunteers supplement operations, receiving accommodation, meals, and commission-based incentives, reducing labor costs and building community engagement.
